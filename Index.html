Today, we saw different traditional weapons and hunting tools used for catching animals and fish. Each one had a specific purpose, made from materials people found in nature. Spears (bangkaw), bows and arrows, and blades like bolos and knives were used for hunting. Some were thrown from a distance, while others were used up close. There were also traps and snares made from wood and rope, designed to catch animals without having to run after them. For fishing, we saw bubu, cone shaped fish traps made from bamboo. Some people even used plant-based poison to make fish float to the surface, making them easier to grab.

We had the opportunity to view and photograph traditional musical instruments. They were made from bamboo, wood, and metal, and each one had its own design and purpose. As we looked around, one of us pointed out the gongs big, round, and solid, as if they could fill an entire space with sound. The other focused on the drums, carefully made from wood and animal skin, looking strong and well used. We also noticed the bamboo flutes.
